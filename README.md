## A Little About Me

I'm Leesha — an independent AI safety researcher who likes turning weird LLM behavior into experiments people can actually understand.

I spend a lot of time poking at prompt injection, model behavior, and the gap between what an AI system *seems* to understand and what it actually does under pressure.

My recent builds are [Prompt-Safety-Classifier](https://github.com/leeeshart/Prompt-Safety-Classifier) and [PromptSentinel](https://github.com/leeeshart/PromptSentinel). I'm also leading Helix at [Synthica](https://www.linkedin.com/company/synthica-org/), a research chapter for high school and college students who are curious but don't know where to start.

If even one person walks away thinking differently about research, that's enough for me.

<img src="./img3.png" width="25%" align="right" alt="Pink decorative character art" />

## Research / Builds

- **Prompt injection + LLM safety:** I like finding the tiny instruction cracks that become big system failures.
- **Human psychology + AI behavior:** I care about why people trust models, when they shouldn't, and how interfaces shape that trust.
- **Student research communities:** Helix is my way of making research feel less gatekept and more like something you can start by asking better questions.
- **Documentation as a personality trait:** I build, break, write down what happened, and keep the failures in the story.
